Town of Beryl Junction

History of Beryl Junction, UT

Originally established as a railroad siding, Beryl lies nine miles northwest of New Castle and 13 miles north of the Junction. Beryl got its name in 1901 from the semi- precious stone Beryl found in the area. A New Castle company in 1905 built a hotel in Beryl in an attempt to get people to move out there, but they quickly went out of business.

Over the years, the community here in Beryl has grown as agricultral technology has progressed, making the dry, dusty Escalante Desert farmable and productive.

Statistics

As of the 2010 census, the population of Beryl Junction proper was 197 people, and of the 70 housing units at the Junction, 99 were occupied.

As of 2012, the Beryl zip code 84714 population was 944, with a population density of 2 per square mile.

Beryl Junction has an elevation of over 1 mile!
