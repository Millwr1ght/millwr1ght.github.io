Ode to Procrastination

Long ago, the tinkerer races of gnomes crafted machines, mechanisms and magics in the shade-filled meadows of the forest of Stroughton Tree. Stroughton Tree was a place known for wonderful archives of learning and knowledge, guarded by a people who sought to preserve wisdom for future generations. Built in a desert oasis, it was to be an oasis of knowledge in a desert of war and famine.

As kingdoms and empires rose and fell around them, the gnomes of Stroughton Tree recorded their neighbors' histories and tracked their neighbor's cultural achievements. The Grand Branches Archives of the gnomes housed ages of sciences, arts, cultures and history. They were a wonder of the world, sought out by artificers and artisans, by wizards and wanderers, by the more and less fortunate. Many who visited stayed, basking in the bounties of this desert fountain.

After many centuries of relative peace and prosperity, the roots of Stroughton Tree were blighted. Either the mines dug too deep, or the explorers reached too far, or any other reason, it all remains the same: the roots of their tree of knowledge died. Barbaric invaders of all sorts sought out the tree, not for it's riches of technology and art, but for its gold and jewels. A fatal plague brought about an end to many great scholars, warriors, and tinkerers. Disease. Famine. War. With the fountain of understanding dammed by those who understood naught, the oasis' flowing rivers dried up.
