Design Principles

Nathan Johnston

Repetition

YouTube does a great job of using repetition in their mobile site design. You can tell where each video card begins and ends because each one is has the same elements: a title card, the video creator, and the video's title, duration and view count information. Not only does it help users recognize separate videos, but it makes for a cleaner site.

Contrast

This is a sceencap of the Quad Cities travel website home page. Here you can see the bright, contrasting colors they used for their site design. The different blues and oranges used are consistent throughout the site, and provide great contrast for the white text above them. Everything on this site is readable and clear.

Alignment

This is the front page of krunker.io, a FPS browser game. Typically it would be played on a desktop or laptop computer, but here we have it loaded up on a mobile device. All the game's options and extras are left-aligned with each other along the edge of the screen. This use of alignment is better on the eyes and makes it easier for users to find what they're looking for.
